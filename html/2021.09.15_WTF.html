Honestly this is baffling. Say you have three Scheme files. Each with macros.

; one.scm (define (one) "one")
; two.scm (include "one") (print (one)) (define-syntax one (syntax-rules () ((one) "two"))) (print (one))
; three.scm (include "two") (one) (define-syntax one (syntax-rules () ((one) "three"))) (print (one))

What gets printed?

./three one two three

At each stage of compilation, a macro rewrites the definition. I'm wondering how to utilize this.

Food for thought.
